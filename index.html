2025BrandingB2B Industrial

They came in asking for a logo. What they needed was a reckoning with what they stood for — and who they were willing to lose to mean something to anyone.

We spent two weeks doing nothing visible: founders, sales, churned customers, loyal ones. Every strong brand has a contradiction at its centre. Theirs sold certainty into an uncertain industry while operating like an experiment. We built the identity on that gap instead of papering over it.

They didn't redesign our logo. They told us who we were and dared us to mean it. Sales stopped explaining what we did within a quarter.
— CEO, Atlas Foundry

2025CopywritingDTC Outdoor

The old site read like every premium outdoor brand: crafted, intentional, built for the long haul. Words anyone could write. Words no one remembers.

A week in the warehouse made it clear: people weren't buying gear, they were buying permission to take themselves seriously outside. That became the voice — direct, impatient, allergic to filler. The about page is 47 words. The returns page is six.

Every page sounds like us now — sharper than us, honestly. Conversion lifted 28% and people actually reply to the emails.
— Founder, North & Vector

Confidential

2024ScalingUnder NDA

A Series B SaaS company — 60 people, two product lines, four exec misfires in 18 months. Not a strategy problem. A cadence problem. Decisions weren't made; they were deferred until the calendar made them.

We installed a rhythm: weekly metric review, biweekly hiring loop, monthly bet sizing. Cut leadership from eleven to six. Net revenue retention moved 102% → 121% in four quarters. Then we left the building.

We brought them in to fix strategy. They fixed how we made decisions, which turned out to be the actual problem.
— CEO, Series B SaaS (Under NDA)

About

One consultant. Every engagement. No hand-offs.

Killing Floor runs on a simple premise: the work that actually moves companies — positioning, voice, operating structure — gets diluted the moment it passes through layers. So it doesn't. Every client works directly with the principal from kickoff to handoff.

Background spans brand strategy, B2B and DTC copywriting, and operating design across industrial, SaaS, and consumer. Agency-side, then operator-side, then here — the only order that makes sense.

Based in Vancouver. Working with founders everywhere.

05

Process

12-week sprint
Week 0Fit call, scope lock, kill list. We agree on exactly one thing to make true and everything we won't touch.
Weeks 1–4Discovery. Competitor mapping, customer interviews, internal audit. We find the contradiction at the centre before any output.
Weeks 5–8Build. Positioning locked, identity or copy or systems drafted and tested. First real output ships at week six.
Weeks 9–12Rollout, refinement, handoff. Operating rhythm installed. We leave with something running, not a deck explaining what to do next.
AfterNo retainer. No scope creep. If something breaks in month four, we talk. That's different from being on call forever.
